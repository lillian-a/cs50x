Codes & Ciphers

Crytography is the process of writing or reading secret messages or codes. The Caesar Cipher and Vigenere Cipher are two enciphering codes that can be used. They take 'Plaintext' (words in readable language) to 'Ciphertext' (encoded words). Below you can learn more about them and even create your own secret codes. Have Fun!

Caesar Cipher

The Caesar Cipher is a simple substitution cipher in which each letter is replaced by another letter a fixed number of positions down the alphabet. The image above show a shift of 5 positions. In this A becomes F, B becomes G and so on. To use the Encrypter and Decrypter below, enter the text you want to Encode into the Plaintext box, choose a number to 'shift' by, and click the Encrypt button. To Decrypt, enter the text into the Ciphertext box, choose a number to 'shift' by, and click the Decrypt button.

Vigenere Cipher

The Vigenere Cipher is a polyalphabetic substitution cipher that uses a number of different Caesar Ciphers based on letters of a keyword. Above is the basic chart the Vigenere Cipher uses. If you take the plaintext letter 'I' and then the keyword letter 'K' the ciphertext letter will be 'R'. To use the Encrypter and Decrypter below, enter the text you want to Encode into the Plaintext box, enter the text of a keyword to use and click the Encrypt button. To Decrypt, enter the text into the Ciphertext box, enter the text of a keyword to use, and click the Decrypt button.
